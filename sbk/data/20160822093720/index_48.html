                      Benjamin Nead Duncan Smith • a year ago

                      Perhaps I can count myself lucky that I simply didn't have that sort of cash on hand back then to make a deposit. Even if I did, my typical inclination on something like this is to wait until the first production batch is already out there and see what actual customers are saying about it. What I figured was that, by summer 2014, there would be all sorts of user reviews on the web.

                      Such a shame that it's taken this long, that nothing is out there and sorry to hear your one of the many who has been waiting for so long. The one bit of good news I've gathered from reading the YouTube comments is that Superpedestrian does actually provide refunds (for now, at least.)

                      What's ironic is that the competition, the FlyKly, was given so much neutral or even bad press in the late 2013 to early 2014 time period, with accusations that they stole the Copenhagen Wheel design and/or technology. But that product changed dramatically when they partnered with the Italian Zehus company in mid 2014.

                      Flyky also had delays beyond what they initially promised on their online funding web page and they took some heat from investors when the design was revamped by the Zehus collaborators. But FlyKly was legitimately delivering their product as of around January 2015 and real world reviews have been very positive.

                      Even here, though, most online reviewers are cautioning their readers to "wait for the Copenhagen Wheel before buying the FlyKly, so side-by-side comparisons can be made." Thus, I'm going to guess that this is holding some people back on the FlyKly at this point. Since I'm now riding a 120mm rear dropout single speed, the FlyKly (which matches a bike like that) is looking better all the time.
